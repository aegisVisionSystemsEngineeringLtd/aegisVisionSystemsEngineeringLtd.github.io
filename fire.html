Understanding Fire Alarm System Basics

Fire alarm systems consist of various components designed to detect and alert occupants to fire or smoke hazards. Understanding the specific types and key components will help you choose the right system for your building. AegisVision provides professional installation, testing, and ongoing maintenance to ensure full compliance with NFPA standards and local Ghanaian fire safety regulations.

Types of Fire Alarm Systems

Each system is tailored for different environments and needs

Conventional Fire Alarm System

Zoned detection · Cost-effective · For smaller buildings

Conventional systems divide the building into zones, each monitored by detectors. When a detector activates, the control panel indicates which zone is in alarm. They are easy to install, cost‑effective, and suitable for small businesses, clinics, and residential properties. Maintenance is straightforward, making them a popular choice for limited budgets.

Addressable Fire Alarm System

Device‑level identification · For large facilities

Addressable systems allow individual addressing of each detection device. When an alarm is triggered, the panel pinpoints the exact device responsible. This enhances reliability and response time, making them ideal for hotels, hospitals, corporate offices, and schools where detailed monitoring is crucial. They can be integrated with other safety systems.

Hybrid Fire Alarm System

Combines conventional & addressable · Flexible migration

Hybrid systems integrate existing conventional devices with newer addressable technology. This allows you to enhance your current setup without full replacement — saving costs while improving safety. Particularly effective for buildings undergoing renovation or expansion.

Wireless Fire Alarm System

Radio communication · Easy retrofitting

Wireless systems use radio signals instead of cabling. They are easier to install, ideal for heritage buildings, schools, and temporary structures where wiring is impractical. They offer similar capabilities to wired systems and can be easily expanded.

Key Components of Fire Alarm Systems

The building blocks of reliable fire safety

Fire Alarm Control Panel (FACP)

The brain of the system

The control panel processes signals from detectors and initiates alerts. It displays alarms, faults, and system status. Available from 2 to 32 loops with LCD display and battery backup. Programmable for building‑specific logic and integration with BMS or smoke management systems.

Request Quote

Smoke Detectors

Ionisation · Photoelectric · Multi‑sensor

Smoke detectors sense airborne particles from fire. Ionisation types respond quickly to fast‑flaming fires, while photoelectric are better for smouldering fires. Multi‑sensor units combine both technologies for maximum reliability. Ideal for corridors, bedrooms, and common areas.

Request Quote

Heat Detectors

Fixed temperature · Rate‑of‑rise · Industrial grade

Heat detectors activate when temperature exceeds a fixed threshold or rises too quickly. They are ideal for kitchens, boiler rooms, and dusty environments where smoke detectors may false alarm. Reliable and maintenance‑friendly.
